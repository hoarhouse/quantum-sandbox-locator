What Is a Quantum Sandbox?

Quantum sandboxes are collaborative hubs where emerging quantum technologies are developed, prototyped, and scaled. They are backed by federal programs (DOE, NSF, CHIPS Act) and offer critical infrastructure to innovators.

Types of Quantum Sandboxes

  • Quantum Networking: Networks and secure communication (QKD)
  • Quantum Materials: Superconductors and quantum-compatible materials
  • Quantum Sensing: Precision measurements and sensor technologies
  • Quantum Computing: Experimental and scalable QC platforms

Who Can Participate?

  • Startup Founders: Access via partnerships, SBIRs, and CRADAs
  • Academic Researchers: Grants, direct NSF collaboration, and consortia
  • Corporate Innovators: Joint research and pre-competitive programs

Getting Started

Engagement typically begins with a proposal or collaborative agreement:

  • DOE Quantum Infrastructure FOAs
  • NSF Quantum Leap Challenge
  • SBIR/STTR programs and direct lab calls
