What Is a Quantum Sandbox?

Quantum sandboxes are experimental environments funded by DOE, NSF, CHIPS Act, and NIST. They enable collaboration across academia, industry, and government, supporting innovation in quantum computing, networking, sensing, and materials.

Types of Quantum Sandboxes

Quantum Networking

Focused on developing secure quantum communication and quantum key distribution (QKD).

  • Example: Fermilab Quantum Network Testbed (DOE)
  • Breakthroughs: QKD experiments, scalable quantum networks

Quantum Materials

Research into novel materials essential for quantum technologies.

  • Example: MIT Quantum Foundry (NSF)
  • Applications: Superconductors, quantum computing components

Quantum Sensing

Advancing precision measurements and sensor technologies.

  • Example: Sandia National Labs Quantum Sensors (DOE)
  • Impact: Navigation, biomedical devices, infrastructure monitoring

Quantum Computing

Exploration of various quantum computing platforms and architectures.

  • Example: Oak Ridge Quantum Computing Facilities (DOE)
  • Focus: Trapped-ion, superconducting qubits, topological quantum computers

Who Can Participate?

  • Startups: Engage via Cooperative R&D Agreements (CRADAs), SBIR grants, or tech-transfer initiatives.
  • University Researchers: Join through NSF grants, lab collaborations, and consortia.
  • Corporate Innovators: Partner in pre-competitive research programs or direct-access models.
